#23 The Spinning Plates...

20 January 2019


My second week as a Junior Software Engineer is complete and I am enjoying it so far. The work I am doing is not exactly the web development(.NET Core, React) that I am eventually hoping for. However, it is a great experience and I am continuing to prepare for other development opportunities that might become available in the future. I have really been focused on learning C# and .NET Core fundamentals but recently I have just began to shift some focus back to JavaScript and React.


I am not abandoning C# and .NET Core learning but I want to stay fresh and relevant on all the technologies I may be involved with. Recently, I felt the need to really dive into those but now I need to ensure I keep my knowledge/skills up to date with JS/React as well. This company has both .NET Core and React developers and I know of a possible opportunity outside this company which involves React. I am not sure what the future holds but I need to be ready for whatever is thrown my way. It is tough to decide what to learn and it feels like I have so many different things spinning at once.


This weekend I decided to begin converting my personal website to use React. It is not really necessary, but it will give me something to jump back into React with and I do have a few ideas/changes to try during the conversion. Now that I have started, I am already feeling more confident by the minute and doing some front-end work again is kind of fun. Not sure when it will be completed but it will be a side project I will be working on during my off-time.


In addition to converting this website to React, I am also trying to read more. I just started a popular book by the name of "The Pragmatic Programmer". by Andrew Hunt and David Thomas. I'm only one chapter in and it seems like a solid book with --- you guessed it -- lots of practical advice about the world of software development. I will also continue to study C#, .NET Core and review the code that the more experienced developers have written to continue to wrap my head around that paradigm. Also, every opportunity I get to talk with the .NET Core developers I will. The short time I have had to talk to them, they have given some great tips while also answering some curiosities I had about the technology. At the moment, I do not work direct with the React developers but I will attempt to engage with at least one of them soon. It's a balancing act trying to learn and retain all I can about everything, but for now that is what makes it interesting.


-Kent-

"Step with care and great tact, and remember that life's a great balancing act."
