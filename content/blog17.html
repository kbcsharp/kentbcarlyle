#17 Hills and valleys...

13 October 2018


If you are going to go through a coding bootcamp, definitely be prepared for highs and lows. At times I have felt like I know so much and I'm on top of the world. Next day, I feel like I don't know anything and wonder how in the heck is anyone else a developer -- because it is just too tough with way too many things to know. After completing Gold badge, I felt like I was on top of the 'coding' world. Once Blue badge began and React and Node got thrown in the mix, I went from hero to zero.


This week, after being in a never-ending valley the last two to three weeks, I can finally see the top of a hill again. During orientation we were given the chart shown above. It is definitely a chart that mirrors reality. However, I feel like it should have multiple small hills and valleys before the final acsension. I feel like the last few weeks I have thought I was on the upswing only to get knocked back down into a valley. Maybe there isn't really a final acsension, maybe you just go through the cycle over and over. Instead though, the valleys just get smaller and smaller. Plus, you start going through it so many times, you know to be more patient and you realize that the light at the end of the tunnel does exist.


This past weekend I rewrote my backend code for like the 4th time. I really wanted to understand everything as well as possible and get the finished code just how I wanted. So far this week it appears to have paid off and I was able to focus solely on the front-end as planned. What wasn't planned was at every turn there seemed to be an issue that I just couldn't solve right away. React is a great library and can be powerful in the right hands. However, it can quickly get confusing with so many different components and prop passing. I gained a lot of valuable experience and knowledge this week though.


All of the different problems I ran into have solutions. Going through the struggle of finding the solutions that work and the experience of seeing those solutions firsthand is invaluable. I can draw from these experiences and remember certain patterns that come with those solutions. Also, there is nothing better than running into a coding problem with your app and then finally solving it on your own.


Whether in life, your profession, or like me in this programming bootcamp -- It would be great to always be on top of the mountain. However, that is just not how things work, it's not reality. Everyone needs those trials and troubles to get to the top. The tough times make you stronger and able to get where you ultimately want to go. Most importantly, they give you an apprecation for what you earn when you finally arrive at the 'destination'.


-Kent-
