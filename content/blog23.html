#23 The Software Engineer Intern...

13 January 2019


I was fortunate enough to be offered the Junior Software Engineer position I was going for at J.D. Byrider. I accepted the position and I have finished my first week on the job. I know they had many applicants to choose from, so I am very grateful to be given this opportunity. I am very excited to begin working in an Agile environment and to be able to see firsthand how the work-center operates. They ended up hiring myself and another intern. The other intern is a CS student who is very close to graduating. We are in different stages of our life so this should be a lot of fun and make the experience even more valuable than it already is.


This first week we got settled in and received some training with our primary tasks. We have also been active participants in daily stand-ups as well as sprint planning. Everyone is really nice and helpful, the work environment is really great. It seems the company is really tech focused, so that bodes well for the developers. I am glad that I focused on SQL and C# to prepare as best I could. Especially SQL, since we really didn't directly interact with the Database during my training, it was vital that I took those two SQL courses.


I don't think it is a good idea to get too specific with what we are working on, but I will say we are primarily dealing with forms and databases. Most of what I have done previously is the web development aspect(e.g. React, Node), so this has helped my expand my knowledge and see more of the complete picture somewhat. The first week is over and I didn't delete a production database like this poor guy, Accident DB Deletion. Therefore, I would say it has been a good week, haha.


I do hope to get back to some more of the web development aspects eventually, so for now I am continuing to expand my C# knowledge primarily with continuing to go through the C# Player's Guide Book. It is definitely easier learning C# since I am fairly proficient in JavaScript. However, I still have so much to learn about C# and even JavaScript for that matter. However, I feel like I have a solid base to begin ASP.NET Core. I could spend until the end of time learning every detail of a language but once you feel that you have some kind of decent foundation, that is usually the time to start building something. You never feel like you are ready anyway, so might as well start. Now that I am learning ASP.NET Core, as I move along I will run into Entity framework and many other things that I can dive into and learn more as I go. In comparison to Node, so far it seems ASP.NET is more structured with it's middleware and I like that. Still, it definitely helps having that previous experience with Node.


I know many people coming out of a coding bootcamp don't want an internship and really just want a "real" job right out of the gate. Then again, I know some people just aren't able to afford to take an internship position. Fortunately, for me, I have been given hours that will allow me to gain great experience while still being able to run my eBay business. Therefore, I can make it work financially. If you are able to get an internship and can afford to take the lower pay, do it! The experience is invaluable. Nothing beats experience! Especially, in the software development/engineering space. I am so fortunate to have this position and I look forward to making the best of it. Hopefully, this is my golden ticket to a great software development/engineering career.
